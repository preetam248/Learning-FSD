Swami Vivekananada

Swami Vivekananda was a pioneering Indian monk who introduced Vedanta and Yoga to the West, emphasizing religious unity, self-realization, and service to humanity.

About the Legend

  • Birth: January 12, 1863, Kolkata, India (born as Narendra Nath Datta).
  • Disciple of: Sri Ramakrishna, 19th-century saint and mystic.
  • Spiritual Mission: Spread the teachings of Vedanta and Yoga in India and the West.

Key Contributions

  • Introduced Hindu philosophy (Vedanta and Yoga) to the Western world.
  • Philosophical Focus:
    • Advaita Vedanta: Non-dualistic philosophy emphasizing the unity of the soul (Atman) with the ultimate reality (Brahman).
    • Service to Humanity: "Service to man is service to God."
    • Religious Universalism: All religions lead to the same truth; promoted tolerance and unity.
  • Chicago Speech (1893): Gained global recognition at the Parliament of the World's Religions; emphasized religious harmony.
  • Founded the Ramakrishna Mission (1897): Focused on social service, education, and spiritual development.

Key Messages

  • "Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."
  • Promoted strength, unity, and self-reliance.
  • Believed in the potential of the individual and the power of inner growth.
